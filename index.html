The root structure of the problem

Illiteracy is not just about reading.
It is about access to science.

Literacy is the prerequisite for every other kind of learning. Before a student can engage with a physics equation, a chemical formula, or a biological concept, they must be able to decode written language. 763 million adults worldwide cannot.

Even among those who are literate, science education remains inaccessible. Traditional textbooks present static diagrams. Lectures describe motion in words. Neither approach lets a student see Newton's laws happen, observe a pendulum's period change, or manipulate variables in a projectile arc.

The result: science is perceived as abstract, memory-based, and disconnected from observable reality. Students disengage. Concepts that could be understood in minutes through direct interaction take weeks of rote repetition — if they are learned at all.

Where this project focuses

Three subjects. Universally taught.
Universally difficult to visualise.

Physics
Motion, forces, energy, and oscillations are dynamic phenomena. A static diagram of projectile motion cannot convey how range depends on angle. A printed pendulum cannot show how period depends on length. Interactivity replaces description with direct observation.

The response to this problem

Interactive simulation as
a teaching instrument.

This project is a browser-based, zero-cost, zero-installation science education platform. Any student with a smartphone and internet access — regardless of language, income, or access to a laboratory — can directly manipulate the variables that govern physical, chemical, and biological systems and observe the outcomes in real time.

🎮
Interactive simulations, not static content
Students change angle, mass, gravity, spring constant, or length and see the physics update instantly. The relationship between variable and outcome is observable directly, not described abstractly.
🧮
Integrated problem solver with worked steps
A built-in physics solver covers kinematics, Newton's laws, energy, SHM, circular motion, and projectile motion — showing full working, not just answers. Students learn method, not just result.
🔁
Unit converter across 18 physical categories
From base SI units to imperial, astronomical, and derived units — covering length, time, speed, force, energy, pressure, temperature, and more. Removes the unit-conversion barrier that stops students engaging with real problems.
🌐
Bilingual: English and Bengali
Full interface translation into Bengali addresses one of the largest linguistically underserved student populations — over 230 million native speakers — who encounter science education exclusively in a second language.
How this addresses the documented gaps
01
No lab required. All nine motion simulations run in a browser. A school with no equipment can still let students observe centripetal acceleration, SHM, and projectile trajectories with real parameter control.
02
No installation, no cost. The platform runs on any device with a browser. The barrier to access is a URL — nothing else.
03
Correct physics throughout. All simulations use real-time deltaTime and SI units. The HUD always shows accurate values. Students are never shown approximate or illustrative-only numbers.
04
Covers the examined curriculum. Every simulation and solver formula maps directly to secondary and higher-secondary physics syllabi used across South Asia, the Arab states, and sub-Saharan Africa — the regions with the largest science education deficits.
